# Supplemental Reference: Drip Campaigns & Automation

Use this reference document when generating multi-email sequences, autoresponders, or triggered customer journeys.

## 1. Trigger Logic & Types
Identify the exact action that initiates the journey. Scale the journey to the opportunity.
*   **Action-Triggered**: Sent in response to a user action (e.g., cart abandonment, signing up for a free trial, downloading a lead magnet).
*   **Inaction-Triggered**: Sent in response to a lack of activity (e.g., win-back email for no purchases in 6 months).
*   **Date-Triggered**: Sent based on calendar dates (e.g., birthdays, renewal reminders).

## 2. Standard 30-Day Autoresponder Sequence
For a generic welcome or onboarding series, recommend sending messages anywhere from two to four days apart to maintain engagement without burning out the list. Use this structural guideline:

*   **Day 1**: Welcome email. Deliver the lead magnet or promised incentive immediately.
*   **Day 2**: Highlight the website's "best-of" or highest-performing content.
*   **Day 4**: Introduce the company's core products and services.
*   **Day 6**: Ask subscribers to add the sender email address to their safe sender list (whitelisting).
*   **Day 8**: Provide a piece of pure educational content or training material.
*   **Day 10**: Cross-promote (invite subscribers to follow on social media or join an SMS list).
*   **Day 14**: Direct promotion of a product or service.
*   **Day 20+**: Continue alternating between educational content, affiliate promotions, and direct company discounts.

## 3. Automation Flow Rules
*   **The Journey-Ending Action**: Define what stops the automated sequence. (e.g., If a subscriber buys the product on Day 4, they must automatically be removed from receiving the Day 14 promotional email).
*   **Frequency Caps**: Ensure subscribers do not receive conflicting triggered messages simultaneously. Establish a hierarchy (e.g., a cart abandonment email takes precedence over a standard browse abandonment email).
*   **Varying Content**: Avoid repeatedly sending the exact same warning email. If a user abandons a cart multiple times, test different subject lines, lengths of inactivity, and discount sizes as the journey progresses.
